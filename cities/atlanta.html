How Many Porta Potties Do You Need in Atlanta?

OSHA Requirements for Construction Sites

For construction sites in Atlanta and throughout Georgia, OSHA mandates:

  • 1-20 workers: One toilet seat
  • 20-200 workers: One toilet seat and one urinal per 40 workers
  • 200+ workers: One toilet seat and one urinal per 50 workers

Service Frequency: OSHA requires toilets be serviced weekly minimum. For high-traffic sites (50+ workers), many Atlanta contractors opt for twice-weekly service.

Event Recommendations

General rule: One standard porta potty per 50 guests for events up to 4 hours.

  • 50 guests: 1-2 units

Atlanta Porta Potty Regulations & Permits

City of Atlanta Permit Requirements

For most residential and commercial properties in Atlanta, porta potty placement does not require a specific permit. However, permits ARE required for:

  • Public property: Parks, sidewalks, public parking - requires Special Event Permit from Atlanta Department of Parks & Recreation
  • Street closures: If porta potties block traffic - requires Atlanta DOT permit
  • Historic districts: Virginia-Highland, Inman Park, Grant Park may have additional HOA requirements

Placement Restrictions in Atlanta

  • Cannot block sidewalks (ADA compliance - 36" minimum clearance)
  • Must be 10+ feet from property line (unless written permission from neighbor)
  • Cannot block fire hydrants, fire lanes, or emergency vehicle access
  • Must be on level ground and accessible by delivery truck

Pro Tip: Your porta potty rental company will handle most compliance issues. Patriot Portables and LDR Site Services both have dedicated staff who know Atlanta regulations.
