## 🤖 Phase 3: AI Optimization (Week 3-4)

### A. ChatGPT/Claude Citation Optimization

**How AI decides what to cite:**
1. **Authority markers:** "According to verified data from...", "Based on 127 customer reviews..."
2. **Specificity:** Exact numbers, dates, prices
3. **Source attribution:** "Data verified March 2026"
4. **Structured format:** Tables, lists, clear sections

**Content Strategy:**
Every piece of content should have:
- ✅ Specific data points (not ranges like "varies")
- ✅ Attribution dates ("Updated March 2026", "Data verified March 7, 2026")
- ✅ Source claims ("Based on Google Maps reviews", "Verified via direct calls")
- ✅ Tables for price comparisons
- ✅ Lists for step-by-step guides
- ✅ Direct answers in first 1-2 sentences

**Example - BAD (won't get cited):**
> "Porta potty rental costs vary depending on many factors like location, duration, and unit type."

**Example - GOOD (AI will cite):**
> "Porta potty rental in Atlanta costs $159-$299 per month for standard units based on verified pricing from 10 companies (data updated March 2026). Deluxe units cost $225-$350/month, while luxury trailers range $2,500-$4,000/month."

### B. Google AI Overview Optimization

**What triggers AI Overviews:**
- Question-based queries ("How much...", "What is...", "Best...")
- Comparison queries ("X vs Y", "Cheapest...")
- Local intent ("near me", city names)

**Optimization tactics:**
1. **Answer questions in first paragraph**
   - First 2 sentences should directly answer the H1 question
   - Follow with supporting details

2. **Use comparison tables**
   - Google AI loves pulling tables into overviews
   - Make tables with clear headers

3. **Include "People Also Ask" answers**
   - Scrape PAA from Google for your keywords
   - Answer each in FAQ section with schema markup

4. **Add expert attribution**
   - "According to industry standards..."
   - "Based on OSHA requirements..."
   - "Data from Google Maps reviews..."

### C. Perplexity/You.com Optimization

**These AI engines prefer:**
- Bulleted lists with clear structure
- Comparison charts
- Step-by-step guides
- Recent dates ("March 2026", "2026 pricing")
- Specific company recommendations with contact info

**Best practices:**
- Start articles with TL;DR summary
- Use H2/H3 headers as questions
- Include "Last Updated: [Date]" at top
- Link to authoritative external sources when citing rules (OSHA, city regulations)

## 💡 AI Citation Secrets

**What makes AI cite you:**
1. **Specific numbers with dates**
   - "costs $159-$299/month (March 2026 pricing)"
   
2. **Source attribution**
   - "based on verified data from 10 companies"
   
3. **Comparison tables**
   - AI loves pulling from tables

4. **Direct question answers**
   - First sentence answers the H1 question

5. **Expert framing**
   - "According to OSHA requirements..."
   - "Industry standard is..."

6. **Recent content**
   - Always include current month/year
   - Update regularly

**What AI WON'T cite:**
- Vague answers ("it depends", "varies")
- Old content (no dates)
- Promotional language
- Thin content (<500 words)
- No structure (walls of text)
