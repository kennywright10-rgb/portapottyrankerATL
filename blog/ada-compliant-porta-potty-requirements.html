ADA Compliant Porta Potty Requirements in Georgia 2026

Regulations Published 2026-03-06 • Updated March 2026

ADA Porta Potty Requirements for Atlanta Events & Construction Sites

Quick Answer: The ADA requires at least 5% of porta potties to be wheelchair accessible for public events and construction sites. ADA units must be 60" wide, have grab bars, and meet specific clearance requirements. Atlanta rentals cost $250-$400/month.

When Are ADA Porta Potties Required?

Under the Americans with Disabilities Act and Georgia accessibility codes:

Required For:

  • All public events - Festivals, concerts, fairs, markets
  • Construction sites - Any project with public funding or accommodation requirements
  • Commercial properties - Outdoor dining, retail parking lots during renovations
  • Government facilities - Parks, recreation areas, public buildings
  • Educational institutions - School events, university functions

Not Required For:

  • Private residential construction (single-family homes)
  • Private parties on private property (weddings at your home)
  • Small commercial sites with no public access

ADA Porta Potty Specifications

To meet federal ADA compliance, wheelchair-accessible porta potties must have:

Interior Dimensions

  • Width: Minimum 60 inches (standard units are 43-46 inches)
  • Depth: Minimum 56 inches
  • Clear floor space: 60" diameter turning circle for wheelchairs
  • Door width: Minimum 32 inches clear opening

Grab Bars & Fixtures

  • Grab bars: Required on both sidewalls, 33-36 inches from floor
  • Toilet seat height: 17-19 inches (standard is 16-18 inches)
  • Toilet paper dispenser: Within reach (15-48 inches from floor)
  • Hand sanitizer: Mounted at 40 inches from floor

Entry & Access

  • Threshold: Maximum 1/2 inch raised threshold
  • Ramp: Required if ground is uneven (1:12 slope maximum)
  • Door: Must open outward, lever handle (not knob)
  • Signage: International Symbol of Accessibility required

Placement Requirements

ADA units must be placed for maximum accessibility:

Distance from Event/Site

  • Maximum 200 feet from event center or main gathering area
  • Along accessible route - Paved or firm ground, no stairs
  • Well-lit path if used after dark
  • Level ground - No more than 2% slope to entrance

Spacing Between Units

  • 5 feet minimum clearance on at least one side for wheelchair approach
  • 60-inch clear path to entrance
  • Cannot block accessible route to other facilities
